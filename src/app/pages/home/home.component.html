Lazarus Mission

The mission's primary goal is to find habitable planets beyond our solar system to ensure the survival of humanity in the face of Earth's declining resources and environmental issues. It is named after the biblical figure Lazarus, who was resurrected from the dead, symbolizing the potential rebirth of the human race on a new world.

What is our process?

The process of finding habitable exoplanets can be broken down into four major steps, each of which involves a combination of observational techniques, data analysis, and scientific collaboration

Find Planets

Use telescopes to spot planets orbiting distant stars by looking for starlight dimming or the star's wobble.

Study Properties

Analyze the planet's size, distance from its star, and other features to see if it's in the habitable zone.

Check Atmosphere

Examine the planet's atmosphere for key molecules and gases that suggest habitable conditions.

Evaluate & Observe

Assess the planet's habitability and use advanced tools for more detailed observations if promising.
